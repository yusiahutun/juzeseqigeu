Biography

Jesse Jackson was born in Greenville, SC on October 8, 1941. American civil rights activist and Baptist minister who was a candidate for the Democratic presidential nomination in 1984 and 1988. He also served as a shadow U.S. Senator for the District of Columbia from 1991 to 1997. He was with Martin Luther King Jr. when the famed leader was assassinated. He attended a racially segregated high school as a student-athlete, demonstrating skill in baseball and football.
