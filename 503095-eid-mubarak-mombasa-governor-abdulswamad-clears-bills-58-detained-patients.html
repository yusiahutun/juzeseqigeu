Eid Mubarak: Mombasa Governor Abdulswamad Nassir Clears Hospital Bills for 58 Detained Patients

July 17, 2024
blog
  • Mombasa governor Abdulswamad Nassir, in the spirit of celebrating Eid ul-Fitr paid medical bills for at least 58 patients who remained detained at Coast General Hospital
  • The hospital staff raise concern over a high number of patients from poor backgrounds who had not enrolled to the National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF)
  • Nassir said the county was set to rollout a programme to ensure tens of thousands of families in Mombasa are enrolled to NHIF

As Muslims began Eid ul-Fitr celebrations to mark the end of the Ramadan fasting period many faithfuls of the Islam religion across the world extended good deeds to the needy, friends and even strangers in the spirit of the season.

In the spirit of Eid ul-Fitr

In Mombasa, Governor Abdulswammad Sharif Nassir has extended a kind gesture by clearing pending medical bills of 58 patients who remained at the Coast General Hospital over arrears and allowed them to go home and celebrate the season with family and friends.

David Wese, Kennedy Mhindi, Purity Miriam and Eric Mathew are among the 58 recovered patients who benefited from Nassir's good deeds and could not express their gratitude better than send prayers of blessings to the county chief who is serving his first term.

"I'm thankful to the governor for clearing all my bills. I had stayed here for more than two months due to arrears and the bills were increasing. It feels like a holiday for me because I will be free from the hospital despite being poor and unable to raise bills," Wese said.

Miriam whose son was admitted at the facility thanked the governor for being a friend in need and admitted that she regretted having not enrolled to NHIF which would have saved her son the lengthy stay in hospital.

"I don't have a job and did not have NHIF cover. I had to stay with my son here for close to a month despite him having recovered," she said.

Patients detained at Coast General Hospital over bills

Nassir came to the rescue of the patients during his visit to the region's largest public office and found a list of patients who had recovered but remained detained over accumulated medical bills.

According to medical practitioners at the facility explained that majority of the patients at the facility did not have the NHIF medical cover hence raising money has been a problem to them.

"Some patients have remained here for up to two months after they were declared medically fit to go and complete their recovery from home because they were unable to clear accumulated bills. Unfortunately they didn't have National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF) cover and continuing to stay here meant the bills were increasing," said a doctor at the facility.

According to staff who spoke to TUKO.co.ke the patients who have been cleared to go home but remained detained because of arrears were put in room other than the wards they had been admitted to create space for other patients being admitted.

"We are a public hospital and receive patients on a daily basis. Unlike in private setups we admit patients first without demanding money and that is why sometimes we get here when they recover and are unable to pay bills," the medic explained.

Mombasa to enroll poor families to NHIF

The medic said the lack of NHIF cover was a key contributor to patients remaining in hospital after treatment because out of pocket expenditure on healthcare is costly and leaves the patients relying on well-wishers.

Nassir, during the visit to support tens of thousands of families in the county get enrolled to NHIF to ensure they can access healthcare without exhausting their resources.

"In 10 days from now we will rollout a programmes to ensure tens of thousands of poor families are enrolled to the NHIF scheme. This is the sure way to attain the Universal Health Coverage pillar which is the national government's priority goal," the county chief observed.

He acknowledged that the Coast General Hospital was having a shortage of medical practitioners enough to cater for a high number of patients and said that his administration was employing more personnel to improve service delivery to the sick.

Mombasa county employs 30 nurses

"We have employed 30 nurses, 22 medical officers, six pharm technicians and six laboratory technicians at the facility whose appointment is effective today April, 21st, 2023," governor said.

He said that once done with phase one of the enrollment of poor families to NHIF a second phase to cover more residents will be rolled out.

This comes even as the 2022 Kenya Demographics Health Survey established that 74% of Kenyans were not enrolled to any medical cover whether public or private.
