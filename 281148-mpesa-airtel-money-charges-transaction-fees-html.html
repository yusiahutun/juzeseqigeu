Mpesa to Airtel money charges and transaction fees

June 29, 2024
blog

For a long time in Kenya, money service users were able to send or receive money to or from Airtel and Safaricom networks. However, recipients always received a message that was supposed to be presented to an agent for withdrawal not to mention the high Mpesa to Airtel money charges and vice versa. The process was cumbersome thus discouraging many people from sending money across networks. This is bound to change especially with the introduction of the mobile money interoperability. Airtel money and Mpesa users are able to send and withdraw money conveniently now. The transaction fee between the two networks is much lower compared to previous charges. This article will provide money transfer users with information about Mpesa to Airtel money charges and vice versa.

Sending money from Airtel to Safaricom

As long as the money interoperability is available, it will be possible for Kenyans to transfer funds from Safaricom to Airtel and vice versa. The interoperability service is available through USSD. It is however not attached to the SIM ToolKit. This means, therefore, that you will be required to remember some codes while sending funds from Mpesa to Airtel Money. This may not be as easy as accessing the SIM Toolkit or mobile money menu.

How to send money to Tanzania from Kenya

When you send money from Mpesa to Airtel Money, the account balance of the recipient will automatically be updated and the customer can go to an agent and withdraw the funds or use it for other cash transactions. To send money from M-Pesa to Airtel Money, follow these steps:

  • Dial *234#
  • Choose MPesa products- Option 1
  • Enter 98 to go to open more options
  • Select M-Pesa Tuma Popote -option 8
  • Enter the Airtel number you wish to send funds to
  • Enter your PIN

When sending money from Airtel Money to M-Pesa, follow the steps below:

  • Dial *222*1#
  • Choose Language
  • Select option 1 which is “Send money”
  • Select option 2 which is “Send money to Mpesa”
  • Enter recipient’s Safaricom number
  • Enter PIN
  • The press OK to send the funds.

Charges for sending money across the networks

Currently, the charges for sending money from Mpesa to Airtel money and vice versa have reduced by 75 percent. In addition, the transaction fees between the two networks have reduced by between 40 and 75 percent when compared to the previous charges before the introduction of interoperability service.

Consequently, Airtel money charges to Mpesa customers are expected to reduce as approved by the Central Bank of Kenya. Airtel Money to Mpesa charge for Ksh. 1100 is now Ksh. 15. This is a decrease form the previous Ksh. 25. Sending this amount from MPesa to Airtel Money will cost you Ksh. 25. This is also a reduced figure as it was previously Ksh. 48.

Safaricom has also doubled the limit of funds that customer can send money across networks. Previously the limit was Ksh 35,000, but it is now Ksh 75,000. Mpesa customers who were previously charged up to Ksh 303 to send money amounting Ksh 35, 000 are currently being charged Ksh 110 to send funds amounting to Ksh 70, 000.

Airtel charges

The maximum amount you can send from Airtel to Mpesa is Ksh. 70000 just like Safaricom. For amounts between Ksh. 1 and Ksh. 100, the transaction fee is Ksh. 0. From Ksh. 101, the transaction fee will depend on the amount to be sent. For example, For Ksh. 5, 000 the transaction fee will be Ksh. 45 and for Ksh. 50, 000 it will be Ksh. 66. Comparison of charges

It is cheaper to send money from Airtel to Mpesa compared to sending the same amount from Mpesa to Airtel. The Mpesa charges to Airtel money are a bit on the higher side. For example, when sending funds between Ksh. 101 and Ksh. 500 from Airtel Money to Mpesa, it will cost you Ksh. 7 while sending the same amount from Mpesa to Airtel Money will cost you Ksh 11. If you decide to send the maximum amount which is Ksh. 70,000: from Airtel Money to Mpesa sending the amount will cost you Ksh. 66, while the from Mpesa to Airtel will cost you Ksh. 110.
