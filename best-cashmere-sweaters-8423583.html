The 15 Best Cashmere Sweaters for Women in 2024

February 15, 2024
blog

Not to be corny, but a good cashmere sweater feels like a hug — and that’s before we even get into the style benefits. So it’s no surprise that many of us hang onto the same ones for years and develop tried and trusted brands that we’d recommend to anyone. For example, stylist Emily Evans, known for dressing Ashley Graham, Nicole Scherzinger, and Bebe Rexha, says her favorite cashmere sweater is still a black Gucci piece she has from over 10 years ago, while stylist and editor Sally Lyndley is still slipping on cashmere sweaters from The Elder Statesman she chose 15 years ago for their classic colors and shapes.

If you’re curious about cashmere quality and are wondering why certain sweaters ring in at a much higher price point than others, it’s all about the material’s blend, where it was sourced from, the ply count, and, of course, the brand you’re purchasing it from. We’ve broken down those details for you ahead, while also shedding light on the cashmere sweaters that are most in style in 2024. Lyndley tends to go for sheer and silk blends if you want something trendy, and chunky cable knit or Fair Isle à la Jane Birkin for a nod to vintage fashion.

Fashion blogger LaToya Wright and Megababe founder Katie Sturino also weighed in on their top cashmere picks, as well as the details they take note of when shopping for this seasonal staple. Read ahead to learn about the 15 cashmere sweaters we deem the best on the market, then get tips on how to clean and care for your cashmere from industry pros.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Feel: If you’re interested in exploring the different feels of cashmere, Quince’s Cashmere 101 page is a great place to start. Here, you’ll learn why the texture of Grade A, Mongolian-sourced cashmere feels warmer and more luxurious than other types. It’s the finest quality and most wearable, and this is because the harsh winter conditions in Mongolia cause cashmere goats to produce denser hair that is rare. If Lyndley wants to ensure a great feel before purchasing a new sweater, she checks where it was made and the blend. “Is it 100 percent cashmere, or is it a blend of cashmere, and what is it blended with?” she asks herself. Then, she researches once she has her questions answered.
  • Pilling: It’s crucial to remember that all cashmere will eventually pill, no matter the price. However, the higher the quality, the slower the pilling. To prolong the life of your sweater without pilling, use sweater spray and only wash occasionally. Both Evans and Lyndley also recommend cashmere combs, with Evans suggesting N. Peal’s version for $9, and Lyndley sourcing hers from Amazon for $12. (She combs her sweaters whenever they pill, roughly once or twice a season as needed.) Meanwhile, Sturino consistently uses an under-$20 Conair fabric shaver that she swears by to remove pilling.
  • Care: Overall, many sites recommend wearing your sweater five to seven times in between washes and airing it out after each wear. Invest in a cashmere comb and do this regularly after washes. Lyndley usually dry cleans her sweaters at the end of the season and leaves them hanging in the dry cleaner bag until the following season. Avoid machines at all costs, as cashmere fabric becomes fragile when wet. Air drying, meanwhile, allows the piece to maintain its shape and durability. Also, refrain from fabric softeners, as many of these have harsh ingredients and cashmere grows softer with time naturally, so they aren’t necessary. Another way to care for your cashmere according to Lyndley? Wear a T-shirt as a foundational layer so that you can prolong wear before washing.

Your Questions, Answered

What is the best way to store cashmere sweaters?

If you prefer to clean and care for your cashmere on your own, and you don’t already have your sweaters in a dry cleaner bag to await the following season (or perhaps you wear them more frequently than that), fold your sweaters so the hangers do not damage the shoulders. Wright recommends a vacuum-sealed storage bag with mothballs.

What is the best way to clean cashmere sweaters?

If you choose to hand wash your sweaters, Evans advises hand washing in cool water and laying flat to dry away from the sunlight on a towel. Sturino uses The Laundress Wool & Cashmere Shampoo during this process. Lyndley invests in sweater shelves from Amazon and uses Folex Carpet Cleaner for spot cleaning in between combs. Of course, if you’re team dry clean and you have the time to drop off your sweaters and pick them up, trust this is a safe method. 

What are the best brands to splurge vs. save on cashmere sweaters?

The fact is, you should go shopping for a cashmere sweater knowing what ply/density you are after and whether or not you care where your sweater was made. The more affordable the price tag, the faster the pilling will come, which is the biggest difference between high- and low-end cashmere. “I won’t buy cashmere from fast fashion brands anymore because they tend to pill the first time you wear them and just generally become unwearable because you can’t clean them without them completely losing shape, so I think you’ve got to go for that medium brand — $50 or $60 and up — or super high-end,” Lyndley says.

Marks & Spencer, Quince, J.Crew, L.L. Bean, Reformation, Lauren by Ralph Lauren, La Ligne, and Naadam are recommendations for lower-end picks, while The Row, Khaite, Raey, Toteme, Bruno Cucinelli, Tom Ford, Loro Piana, and Gabriela Hearst can be thought of as splurge-worthy. Sturino’s favorite picks are from Jenni Kayne, Ralph Lauren, and Kule, whereas Wright has found great success with 7 for All Mankind. She also frequently shops at Goodwill when she wants to save and is in the mood to sift through racks. Lyndley is devoted to her cashmere from The Elder Statesman. 

If you’re looking for plus-size or petite sizing, try J.Crew, Ralph Lauren, Naadam, La Ligne, and Vince. Finally, want tips for shopping great cashmere at a marked-down price point? Evans says to scour The Outnet in the summer when no one is looking and Lyndley suggests The RealReal for vintage, but advises to check the condition of the piece you’re choosing, as well as the men’s sale rack at department stores.
