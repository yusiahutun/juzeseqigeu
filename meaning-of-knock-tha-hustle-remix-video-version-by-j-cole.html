Meaning of Knock tha Hustle (Remix) [Video Version] by J. Cole

In J. Cole's remix of "Knock tha Hustle," featuring Cozz, the song touches on a range of themes related to perseverance, ambition, and the challenges faced while chasing success. Throughout the song, the artists delve into the struggles they've encountered on their respective paths and their unwavering determination to overcome obstacles and achieve greatness.One standout lyric that captures the essence of the song's overarching theme is in the chorus: "I been in this hood, dawg, for way too long, man, I been quiet for way too long, been patiently waitin', can't wait too long, dawg. I been broke, man, for way too long." This lyric reflects the constant battle against adversity that the artists have faced in their lives. It embodies their refusal to back down, despite enduring long periods of hardship.Another significant lyric that contributes to the song's theme is in J. Cole's verse: "Death to the imposters, label me the king, my dreams could win Oscars, my niggas turned mobsters." This lyric highlights the artists' ambition and unyielding belief in their own abilities. They refuse to accept anything less than greatness, daring to dream big and challenging societal expectations. By comparing their dreams to winning Oscars, they emphasize the value and significance of their aspirations.The song also addresses the need to stay focused and avoid distractions. Cozz touches on this in his verse: "Fuck being fine, girl, you gotta have a brain too." This lyric represents the artists' recognition that in order to achieve their goals, they must surround themselves with individuals who are also driven and intellectually stimulating. It speaks to the importance of having a strong support system and the ability to distinguish between surface-level attractions and meaningful connections.Furthermore, the lyrics discuss the experiences of growing up in challenging environments and the impact of these experiences on their journeys. J. Cole's verse states, "My nigga movin' state-to-state, runnin' late, tryna avoid state troopers, that's the weight watchers." This lyric paints a vivid picture of the circumstances they have had to navigate and reminds listeners that their paths have not been easy. It also serves as a metaphorical representation of the constant vigilance needed to avoid falling into the traps and pitfalls that often come with their environments.Ultimately, the song's themes revolve around the pursuit of success and the resilience required to overcome obstacles on the path to achieving one's dreams. Through their introspective and thought-provoking lyrics, J. Cole and Cozz convey the importance of perseverance, staying true to oneself, and prioritizing personal growth. They encourage listeners to embrace their own unique journeys, not allowing setbacks or societal expectations to derail them from their own hustle. The power of these themes resonates deeply and serves as a reminder to all that success can be attained if one maintains focus, determination, and unwavering faith in themselves.
