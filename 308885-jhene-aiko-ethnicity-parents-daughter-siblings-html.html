Jhene Aiko is an American singer and songwriter. As a singer, she began her career in the early 2000s when she contributed her vocals to the R&B group B2K. Within that time, she has released two studio albums, one collaboration album, and supported many artists in their tours. Some of the artists she has supported include Beyonce, Jay-Z, Snoop Dogg, Wiz Khalifa, Drake, and J. Cole.

Jhene Aiko’s real name is Jhené Aiko Efuru Chilombo, pronounced as Juh-nay Ahh-ee-ko. Born on 16th March 1988, she is a singer and songwriter who specialises in alternative R&B, neo-soul, hip hop, and pop. But it is her vocals, that blend effortlessly in almost every genre, that have made her a well-known figure in the American music industry.

In 2002, the B2K band released their self-titled debut album. The album was a hit and ranked at number two on the Billboard 200 and number one on the U.S. Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs albums chart. It also won the BET Award for Viewer’s Choice.

She dropped her ambitions for music in 2003 after having some differences with her record label Epic Music. At that time, the two parties could not agree on whether the singer should release her debut album titled “My Name is Jhene.” The decision led her to go back to school and complete her studies.

In 2011, she made a comeback and released her first mixtape called “Sailing Soul(s).” The mixtape featured top artists including Miguel, Kanye West, and Drake. Aiko’s first album came out in 2014. She named it Souled Out released by ArtClub International, ARTium Recordings, and Def Jam Recordings. It was an alternative R&B album with a diverse musical style. The album incorporated psychedelic music, neo-soul, R&B, hip hop, and electronica elements.
