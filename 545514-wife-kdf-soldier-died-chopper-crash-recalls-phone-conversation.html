The wife of one of the fatalities in the deadly Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) helicopter crash has shared her last moments with her husband.

Senior Sergeant John Kinyua Mureithi was accompanying KDF Chief General Francis Ogolla and his 12 colleagues when the crash occurred.

He died while receiving treatment.

KDF officer's last call with wife before crash

Grace Wambui revealed to Nation that she spoke to Mureithi by phone exactly nine minutes before the accident.

She said her husband, on the other end of the phone call, enquired about her whereabouts and if she had taken her lunch.

"He asked if I had taken lunch, but I informed him I had not yet because I was still at work. It was around 2:11pm. I asked him the same and he told me was busy at work," Wambui recalled.

Wamboi revealed they had plans for their young family, which would not be fulfilled as their only pillar was a goner.

She could not hold back her tears as the reality of her husband's tragic death hit.

"We had a lot of great plans for our family. It is sad that this would not happen as he is no more. God has taken him before the plans are actualised," she said amid sobs.

The deceased father, Josphat Muriithi, also recalled his last moments with his son.

He revealed that he talked to his son after the crash and was told that he (Mureithi) was in hospital alive.

Their hopes remained high until President William Ruto broke the sad news via a televised state address.

The jade old man cherished his late son's commitment to service the state in his capacity, recalling with much anguish the many times he called on them at their rural home in Kirinyaga.

Deadly KDF chopper crash in Elgeyo Marakwet

Ruto revealed that the ill-fated Kenya Air Force Huey Helicopter carrying 12 people had just left Chesegon, West Pokot county, for Uasin Gishu county.

It came tumbling down in Keben, Elgeyo Marawket county, a few moments after take off.

Besides Ogolla and Mureithi, others who perished were Brigadier Swale Saidi, Colonel Duncan Keittany, Lieutenant Colonel David Sawe, Major George Benson Magondu, Captain Sora Mohamed, Captain Hillary Litali, Sergeant Cliphonce Omondi, and Sergeant Rose Nyawira.
