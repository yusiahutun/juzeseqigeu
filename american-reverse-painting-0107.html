I think my painting was executed on glass (20"W x 16"H). Does the painting have value? Is the frame the painting's original frame?

D.A., Weatherly, Pa.

American reverse painting on glass is a popular technique. This 1915 example depicts a bucolic landscape. The windows of the house are highlighted with mother-of-pearl accents. The repainted Rococo-style frame is not the original; it's too ornate. The original frame would probably have been a simple mahogany veneer that would not have overwhelmed the painting.

Valued at: $150

* The estimates provided are preliminary only and subject to change based on first hand inspection and further research. Appraisal prices refer to an item's fair market value, or what one might expect to pay for an object of similar age, size, color and condition at auction.
