Julie and David Grygla who had triplets using fertility treatment now expecting TWINS naturally

April 02, 2024
blog

A couple who welcomed triplets just seven months ago are now expecting twins.

Julie and David Grygla, from St George, Utah, welcomed Abraham, Brady and Christian in March after undergoing fertility treatments, so they were shocked when they conceived the twins naturally.

It means that, by next May, the couple will have five children under the age of 14 months old.

While Julie, 33, says she worries about how she'll ever get enough sleep or fit five baby seats inside the family's car, she couldn't be happier.

'I'm grateful that they will all have a buddy, all the way through,' she told the Deseret News.

Shock: Julie and David Grygla, from Utah, learned they are expecting twins just months after they had triplets

Growing brood: Abraham, Brady and Christian Grygla, pictured, are going to be big brothers by next May

'They will have each other to get in trouble with, but also to keep each other on the straight and narrow. That is a great comfort to me. And we're just going to have a lot of fun.'

The couple, who are members of the Church of Latter-day Saints, experienced fertility issues after meeting later in life.

At 30, Julie had moved back home after losing her mother and started teaching at Brigham Young University. There, her brother set her up on a blind date with Dave, a family medicine doctor.

Dave, who was 41, said he always wanted a big family but thought his time was running out.

But the couple were soon dating and married in March 2012. They tried to conceive for 18 months before finally falling pregnant with the triplets after undergoing fertility treatment.

'Dave was thrilled when he found out we were having triplets,' his wife remembered. 'He kept saying, "I can't believe it. I can't believe I'm getting the big family I always wanted".'

But Julie admits that she was feeling overwhelmed.

Joy: Julie (with two of her sons) says that being a mom is the toughest but most rewarding job she has had. She explained that the first time around she had been worried but this time is filled with excitement

Big brothers: The boys, pictured, were conceived after 18 months of trying and fertility treatments. But this time around, the couple, who have always wanted a big family, conceived the twins after just a month of trying

'I cried through the first four months of my pregnancy because I was so scared and so shocked and so not up for it,' she told the Deseret News.

When the boys arrived earlier this year, the job was just as tough as she imagined, with constant feeds, pumping milk and fighting to stay healthy herself, she said.

Sure, it's going to be pretty insane for a while, but think about all the fun we're going to have Julie Grygla 

'Those first few months were so hard,' she said. 'But now we play, and I sing songs with them. It's so much more fun... Being a mom is the hardest thing I've ever done, but it is the most rewarding thing.'

As the couple settled into their new life, they decided to leave it up to God to determine whether or not they should have any more children to achieve the big brood they'd always wanted.

They decided to try naturally and, if they had no luck conceiving, they agreed to try fertility treatment again in six to nine months - but there was no need.

After feeling a wave of nausea over the summer, Julie decided to try a pregnancy test and was shocked when it was positive. She broke the news to Dave, 44, immediately.

'I walked out to Dave, my hand shaking, and handed the test to him,' she wrote on her blog. 'He didn't believe me at first, but when I hugged him, he felt how hard my heart was beating. 

Overjoyed: The couple, at their wedding in 2012, met later in life and thought they might not be able to have kids

Happy: . 'We get to have the big family we both always wanted but never thought was possible,' Julie said

'We both started crying. We could not believe the miracle we were witnessing in that moment. 1.5 years it took us to get pregnant with the boys! And BAM! One month and there we were.' 

She wrote on her blog that she immediately knew she was having twins, and a trip to the doctor confirmed it.

'Instead of the shock we felt with the triplets, we felt incredible excitement,' she wrote.

The twins are due in the new year and Julie, who grew up in a home of 11 children, knows it'll be just as tough - and even more fun.   

'Sure, it's going to be pretty insane for a while,' she wrote. 'But also think about all the fun we're going to have. We get to have the big family we both always wanted but never thought was possible.'
