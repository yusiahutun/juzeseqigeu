Alexa Chung wore a really 'ugly' pair of shoes and we are buying them anyway

June 19, 2024
blog

The British model left us reeling with her frog-inspired clogs

    Fashion powerhouse and indie-sleaze fashion icon  Alexa Chung just proved that she still has an eye for design. Her own eponymous label may have sadly folded last year, but rest assured, she still knows how to make a major statement in the outfit department. 

    While most Easter weekends brimmed with fluffy chicks and springy lambs, Alexa's bank holiday favoured another member of the animal kingdom. In a snap shared on her Instagram Stories with her 6 million followers, the 39-year-old rocked some frog-shaped footwear – and we couldn't help but do a double take.

    MORE: Alexa Chung's vintage bag just proved why you should never throw anything away

    READ: Alexa Chung's latest Miu Miu look proves that her trademark style is still unmatched

    The unique amphibian-style clogs are part of a collaboration between British fashion label JW Anderson and quirky heritage rain boot retailer Wellipets. Alexa styled her unconventional footwear with a pair of blue straight-leg jeans – one of the TV presenter's wardrobe staples – allowing her glossy, moss green clogs to take centre stage.

    Each shoe features hand painted details – bulbous yellow eyes and a rich red mouth as well as ladder grip rubber soles. The shoes are available in two other colourways, a deep petrol blue and a bright marigold, all of which retail at £370.

    MORE: Alexa Chung's makeup artist reveals how to get her signature beauty look

    RELATED: Alexa Chung just made it official, ballet pumps are back

    Eagle-eyed fans of Alexa's style will notice that this is not the first time that the fashion icon has incorporated a frog-shaped piece into her repertoire. At London Fashion Week earlier this year, Alexa stepped out with a hyper-realistic piece of arm candy which resembled a frog from JW Anderson's FW23 collection, worn to the label's showcase.

    Honestly? We have so many questions. Clearly, Carrie Bradshaw's pigeon is out and instead the frog is the animal , and we thank Alexa for alerting us. 

    Luckily, no frogs were harmed in the making of these clogs, but we can't say the same for our eyes when we clapped upon the finished result. Polarising and playful, snap these up for spring while they're still in stock, and make a major statement with your footwear this season.
