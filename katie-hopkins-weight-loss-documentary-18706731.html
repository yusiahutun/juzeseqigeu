'Fat people aren't sexy': Katie Hopkins drops three stone to prove obese people are LAZY

August 30, 2024
blog

Queen of controversy Katie Hopkins has speedily shed the weight she put on for a TV documentary to prove 'fat people are lazy'.

Last year the outspoken TV personality caused uproar by saying she 'wouldn't employ obese people because they look lazy' on ITV's This Morning.

And in an effort to prove herself right, she took part in a documentary where she piled on three stone - to show people that being overweight is their own fault.

After gorging on 6,500 calories a day, the 5ft 7in former reality TV star went from a slimline 8st 12 to a hefty 1 1st 13lbs.

The 39-year-old admits that she was reduced to tears when she looked in the mirror at her 'massive gut' and even stopped having sex with her husband Mark Cross.

She told The Sun: "After I put on my first stone, I stopped having sex with my husband.

"As a fat bird, I stuck to my pyjamas and getting naked in the dark.

"A fat belly is not an attractive thing, fat people aren't sexy."

But now the mum-of-three has shrunk back to a skinny size 8 in just a few months and can't wait to tell everyone - in a way that'll do little to win over her critics.

"I fell like I've proved my point," she told Closer magazine. "I fell tougher, fitter, better then before."

"People tell me I'm lucky because I'm 'naturally slim', but I don't agree," she adds.

"We were all born with the same sized bones - if you put too much in and don't move enough, you're going to get fat and I think I've proven that."

Katie, who is mum to India, 10, Poppy, nine and five-year-old Max, had never previously been on a diet and lost her baby weight quickly.

She found it hard to pile on the pounds and had to eat three times the recommended calorie allowance.

Katie was also advised, by the TV Show's nutritionist, that eating lots of high-calorie, high-fat food was the fastest way to gain weight.

The star swapped her normal diet of toast for breakfast, a sandwich for lunch and roast chicken for dinner, for huge portions of chips, chocolate bars and milkshakes.

The TLC documentary even sees ex-army personal trainer Christian devise a military style program of what he terms 'circuit eating' to help her gain the final pounds.

Dividing a table into four, Christian places different foods in each section and makes Katie rotate the table six times, eating the small but high calorie foods at each point.

Katie was monitored to make sure her weight gain had no adverse effects on her health, but found the process very mentally challenging.

"I didn't cry at the birth of my children, I didn't cry at my weddings, but I cried a lot at getting fat!" she added.

In order to lose the excess pounds she had piled on during the show she shunned expensive personal trainers and diet plans.

Instead she opted to go back to her normal routine -running up to four times a week and eating her usual meals of around 1,200 calories a day.

Katie Hopkin: My Fat Story, Fri 2 Jan, 9pm, and Sat 3 Jan, 8pm, TLC
