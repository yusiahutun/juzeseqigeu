The Kitchen's Chambord Layer Cake

May 06, 2024
blog
Ryan Benyi

Rich fruit preserves fill the Kitchen's Chambord Layer Cake with black-raspberry-flavored buttercream.

Yields: 12

Cal/Serv: 869

Ingredients

Save Recipe
  • 1 1/2 c. self-rising flour
  • 1 1/4 c. all-purpose flour
  • 1 c. whole milk
  • 1 tbsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 2 c. unsalted butter
  • 2 c. sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp. heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp. Chambord liqueur
  • 1 tbsp. cranberry juice
  • 4 c. sifted confectioners' sugar
  • 1 c. Chambord black cherry preserves or black raspberry preserves

Directions

  • Step 1Prepare cake pans: Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Lightly coat three 9-inch cake pans with softened butter. Cut three 9-inch circles out of parchment paper and fit them into the bottoms of the cake pans. Lightly coat the paper circles with butter and set aside.
  • Step 2Make the batter: Combine the flours in a medium-size bowl and set aside. Combine the milk and vanilla in another medium-size bowl and set aside. In a large bowl, using a mixer set at medium speed, beat 1 cup butter until smooth -- about 3 minutes.
  • Step 3Gradually add sugar and beat until light and fluffy -- about 3 more minutes. Add the eggs one at a time and beat after each addition -- about 2 minutes.
  • Step 4Reduce mixer speed to low, add half the flour mixture, and beat until incorporated. Add the milk mixture, beat until smooth, add remaining flour, and beat until incorporated.
  • Step 5Bake the cake: Evenly divide batter among the prepared pans and bake on center rack until a tester inserted into the center of each cake layer comes out clean -- about 30 minutes. Cool the cake, in the pans, on a wire rack for 20 minutes. Release the cake from pans and cool completely on the rack.
  • Step 6Assemble the cake: Cream remaining butter in a medium-size bowl, using a mixer set at medium speed, until smooth -- about 3 minutes. Add the cream, Chambord, and juice and beat until smooth. Gradually add the confectioner's sugar, beating continuously, until well incorporated.
  • Step 7Place a cake layer on a serving plate and spread 1/2 the preserves over it. Top with the second layer and the remaining preserves. Place the third layer on top and ice the sides and top of cake with the frosting.
