Gigi Hadid Wears Blue Emilia Wickstead Caped Jumpsuit to Variety's Power of Women in NYC

July 04, 2024
blog
  • Gigi Hadid turned heads in NYC in a bright blue Emilia Wickstead caped jumpsuit from the designer's Fall 2019 collection while attending Variety's Power of Women Event today.
  • She also posed with other monochromatically dressed models at the event, sister Bella Hadid and Ashley Graham.

Gigi Hadid yet again turned the sidewalks of New York City into her personal runway when she stepped out in a monochrome cerulean look featuring a cape so epic it would make Superman jealous. The 23-year-old model was heading to Variety's Power Of Women event in Manhattan, but stopped to give photographers a twirl (in the rain, mind you) and flaunt her dramatic ensemble.

James Devaney//Getty Images

Gigi's jumpsuit is by Emilia Wickstead, and it first hit the runway at London Fashion Week in February with the rest of the British designer's Fall 2019 collection. The original look included a thin black belt and white pumps, but Hadid (with the help of her stylist, Mimi Cuttrell) put her own spin on the look with matching snakeskin pumps and gold hoop earrings.

Raymond Hall//Getty Images

Jeff Spicer/BFC//Getty Images

Shop Emilia Wickstead

Inside the venue, Gigi continued to work her caped outfit on the red carpet, and even teamed up with sister Bella Hadid for a few photos. It looks like the supermodel siblings both took the monochrome route today, as Bella opted for a brown leather suit and coordinating strapless top.

Mike Coppola//Getty Images

Gigi also linked up with Ashley Graham, who was monochromatically dressed in a canary yellow wrap dress and sandals.

ANGELA WEISS//Getty Images

Hadid is featured in Variety's Power of Women issue, along with Kacey Musgraves, Taraji P. Henson, Bette Midler, and Christiane Amanpour. In her cover story with the magazine, Gigi teared up while discussing how she's dealt with fame.

"I’ve had my ups and downs with it for sure, because overall, it’s definitely a learning process," she said. "I’ve learned a lot about myself because of it. I’ve learned to be honest with myself and celebrate myself, protect myself, be assertive when I need to."

Hadid later added, "It makes me emotional because at times, fame makes you feel out of control of your life. I think it’s tough. Obviously, people judge you. People can create a headline or an opinion about someone based on a small moment or a mistake."
