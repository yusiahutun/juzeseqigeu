What is Rational Emotive Behaviour Therapy (REBT)?

April 29, 2024
blog
APA Dictionary of Psychology defines REBT or Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy as a form of cognitive behaviour therapy that is based on the idea that an individual’s self-defeating beliefs influence and may even create negative feelings and behaviours. REBT was developed by Albert Ellis in the 1950s. It is an action-oriented approach that aids individuals in dealing with their irrational beliefs and manages their emotions, thoughts, and behaviours more healthily. Once these irrational beliefs are comprehended by the therapist, they provide therapeutic strategies that would aid the individual in developing healthy interpersonal and intrapersonal relationships.
