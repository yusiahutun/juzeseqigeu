All 51 Passengers Onboard Bus Swept Away by Floods in Garissa Successfully Rescued, Kenya Red Cross

February 02, 2024

  • The Kenya Red Cross Society declared the successful completion of a rescue operation on a bus swept away by floods at Tula Bridge near Lager Areli
  • Priority was given to vulnerable groups, including women, children, and the sick, during the rescue operation, ensuring their safety
  • All passengers aboard the affected Umma bus were rescued, but the Red Cross emphasised their ongoing efforts to assist other stranded commuters

Didacus Malowa, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings over three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Garissa - All passengers trapped in an Umma Express Ltd bus swept away by floods at Tula Bridge have been rescued.

How floods swept Umma bus

Police reports indicated that the bus travelling to Nairobi from Wajir had 51 passengers onboard.

Video Emerges Showing How Umma Bus with 51 Passengers Was Swept Away by Floods in Garissa

A spine-tingling footage emerged showing the moment the bus was swept away as it crossed a flooded road.

Upon reaching the Chuma Mrefu area, the bus slipped off the road while attempting to cross a flooded section following a heavy downpour.

From the video, the driver attempted to control the bus as the surging floods washed it away.

Shouting can be heard as the bus is hurtled away and gets stuck around 30 metres off the highway.

All Umma bus passengers rescued

In a statement on Tuesday, April 9, the Kenya Red Cross Society announced the success of the rescue operation.

The society explained the process during the operation, revealing that they gave priority to the sick, women and children.

They added that they are continuing the operation to help road users who were stranded after the road was destroyed.

"All passengers aboard the bus have now been rescued, with priority initially given to women, children, and those who were sick.However, our efforts are ongoing as we continue to rescue other commuters who were stranded due to the road being cut off at Arer near Tulla Bridge, Tana River County," read the statement.

8 killed in Nakuru tragic accident

In other sad news, eight people were confirmed dead following a road accident along the Nakuru-Nairobi Highway.

In the Tuesday, April 9 tragic incident, a van reportedly rammed into a stalled trailer at Ngata Bridge at around 5:30am.

According to the police report, the private van was ferrying at least 15 people to Kisii for an event.
