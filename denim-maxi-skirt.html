10 long denim skirts to buy now

7 For All Mankind

7 For All Mankind

7 For All Mankind's denim maxi skirt is dyed in a light-wash blue that's reminiscent of the loose-fit jeans worn in the Nineties. Lean into it with square-toed shoes and ribbed knitwear up top.

Hush

Hush

Now 20% Off

We'll be the first to admit denim skirts can be restrictive, so we love that Hush's has put comfort first with this design. By forgoing hardware, belt loops, and traditional fly fastenings for a concealed side zip, this piece looks and feels fuss-free.

Paige

For those who prefer a classic take on the denim maxi, go for a straight skirt rather than A-line shapes. Paige's is ideal – and the frayed trims only further the grungy feeling. The high slit tempers the column cut, but also showcases a hint of skin.

Aligne

The full, circle skirt on Aligne's denim maxi creates a voluminous silhouette for a fluid, unrestricted fit. Streamlined, seam details give structure to the undulating hem, which delivers a balance of whimsy and casual comfort.

Mother

Mother has made the most flattering denim maxi skirt: the pencil shape and high-rise waistband will feel relevant for decades to come. The high slits aren't just there to showcase an alluring flash of skin, it makes fitted designs much easier to walk in.

Nili Lotan

Nili Lotan

Nili Lotan's denim maxi is first up for good reason: we were so inspired by the brand's take on the fabric for SS24. This skirt has a softly pleated A-line silhouette that sits at your natural waist and billows beautifully as you walk.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Citizens of Humanity

Citizens of Humanity

Citizens of Humanity's denim skirt is cut from strips of organic cotton that have been stitched together for a deconstructed effect that's subtly rebellious. It sits at your natural waist and the frayed hem complements the edge of glossy black boots.

Fanfare Label

Fanfare Label

Fanfare Label's maxi is made from old jeans that otherwise would have gone to waste – the result is a cool, tonal-blue, patchworked piece that you can't buy off-the-rack. Always handmade to order (which means you need toallow two weeks for delivery), no two pieces from the brand look the same.

Agolde

If you haven't found the perfect pre-loved denim skirt, Agolde's maxi is the next best thing. The light wash, column shape and frayed hems give this piece a cool lived-in feel. Amp up the vintage aesthetic with a tank top this summer.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Balenciaga

Balenciaga

If you don't wear jeans at the best of times, you can still tap into this trend with Balenciaga's jet-black skirt. Cut to sit lower on the hips, this denim maxi has a striking columnar shape that's tempered by the subtle front slit. The blacker the better with pieces like this, so keep washing to a minimum to preserve your purchase and do your bit for the planet.
