Kenyans have taken a swipe at Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC) following an increase in electricity prices.

This followed the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) announcement of a 16.5% increase in electricity prices starting in January 2024.

Why EPRA increased electricity prices

EPRA attributed the increase in electricity prices to the declining shilling against the US dollar, which is currently exchanging at an average rate of KSh 158.3 per dollar.

The regulator hiked the foreign exchange rate fluctuation adjustment charge by 103.7% to a high of KSh 6.46 per unit, up from KSh 3.17.

It also raised the energy charge by 8.7% to KSh 4.33 per unit, up from KSh 3.98 last month.

What Kenyans said about rising token prices

The move raised concerns among Kenyans, who are already grappling with the rising cost of living.

The majority shared screenshots of token price changes in January 2024 compared to previous months in 2023, showing a significant drop in the number of units per KSh 1,000.

"At this rate, someday in the future, KSh 100 will generate only 1 unit for users."
