Deadspin quietly updates viral story that accused young Kansas City Chiefs fan, 9, of wearing 'black

May 18, 2024

A media outlet which caused a furious backlash against a nine-year-old Kansas City Chiefs fan by accusing him of blackface has quietly updated its viral story.

Sports blog Deadspin removed the photo of Holden Armenta from its November 27 story and added a note stating it 'regrets any suggestion we were attacking him'.

Reporter Carron J Phillips accused Armenta of 'racism' for sporting black and red face paint and a Native American headdress at a Chiefs game, despite the fact the youngster is of Chumash descent.

Phillips’ said Armenta had found 'a way to hate black people and Native Americans at the same time', even though the child's face paint was in the Chief's colors.

Now his editor has added a note to the report stating its intended target was the NFL's failure to tackle racist costumes in crowds.

His original article which specifically called out the boy has since been amended to remove any reference or photos of him

'On Nov. 27, Deadspin published an opinion piece criticizing the NFL for allowing a young fan to attend the Kansas City Chiefs game against the Las Vegas Raiders on Nov. 26 wearing a traditional Native American headdress and, based upon the available photo, what appeared to be black face paint, the note reads.

'Unfortunately the article drew attention to the fan, though our intended focus was on the NFL and its checkered history on race, an issue which our writer has covered extensively for Deadspin. 

'Three years ago, the Chiefs banned fans from wearing headdresses in Arrowhead Stadium, as well as face painting that “appropriates American Indian cultures and traditions.” The story’s intended focus was the NFL and its failure to extend those rules to the entire league.'

It comes after Armenta's family threatened to sue if the offending article was not removed. 

Phillips’ story prompted a media frenzy for the young fan, who described the attention he received as, 'scary'.

The piece ran a photo of Armenta in profile which only showed the side of his face with black paint. 

But despite the backlash, he refused to apologize and doubled down on his comments even after it was pointed out that half of Armenta's face was painted red.

 In a now-deleted tweet, the reporter, a senior writer for Deadspin, wrote: 'For the idiots in my mentions who are treating this as some harmless act because the other side of his face was painted red, I could make the argument that it makes it even worse. Ya'll are the ones who hate Mexicans but wear sombreros on cinco.'

His account on X, formerly Twitter, was put on private mode following the criticism.

The Chiefs banned Native American headdresses at their games in 2020. 

Armenta's father Bubba, Holden's dad, said that his son was 'upset' and 'pretty devastated' when he first learned of Phillips' blackface claims.

His mother Shannon Armenta also hit back at Phillips’ claims in a Facebook post, stating that the team appreciated his support and detailing her son's Native American heritage.

Armenta's grandfather Raul is on the board of the Chumash Tribe in Santa Ynez, California, according to the Post Millennial. 

'It's been a whirlwind of comments coming even from other tribes, from tribal members. Some think it's OK. Some think it's not OK. It's a 9-year-old boy supporting his team,' he added.

The Chumash tribe later issued a statement saying it does not support the wearing of tribal regalia, for costume or otherwise.
