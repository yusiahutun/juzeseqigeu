Can Disney Play a Major Role in Child Development?

August 07, 2024

The changes that children go through as they get older are described by child development. Children develop their knowledge, abilities, and behaviour along with their physical growth. In a child’s development, parents and other adults—like grandparents and daycare providers—play crucial responsibilities.

The biological, psychological, and emotional changes that take place in people between the time of birth and the end of puberty are referred to as child development. Early childhood, middle childhood, and late childhood are the three life stages that makeup childhood. The study of children’s conscious and subconscious development is known as child psychology. To comprehend a child’s mental development, child psychologists watch how the youngster interacts with their parents, the outside environment, and themselves.

The developing child possesses all of the amazing complex structures and developmental domains, including values, social and emotional growth, cognitive development, language and literacy development, and motor development. Dramatic play experiences are thriving in each of these areas.

Importance of child psychology

The first three years following birth are a time of tremendous growth and development because a child’s brain forms intricate neural networks at a rate never seen before. The brain has grown to 90% of its adult size by the age of six, as kids continue to take in an incredible quantity of knowledge from their experiences and surroundings. Both positive and negative early experiences set the stage for a child’s future survival, development, health, and well-being. Good early childhood development has been repeatedly linked to improved long-term health outcomes for children as well as increased opportunities, academic success, and economic potential.

The influence of this time on a child’s emotional and social development is especially significant since it is critical to their future self-assurance, interpersonal interactions, communication, sense of belonging to the community, and mental well-being. According to the World Health Organization’s Nurturing Care Framework, parents should keep an eye on and foster their child’s development by concentrating on five main areas:

  • Excellent Health: Parents can promote their children’s health by keeping them safe from hazards in the home and outside, practising excellent hygiene, and utilizing preventative, curative, and promotional healthcare services. By attending to their child’s needs lovingly and appropriately, parents can also safeguard their emotional well-being. Sufficient Nutrition: During a mother’s pregnancy and in the early postpartum period, a child’s body and brain require essential micronutrients for development. Proteins and different nutrients should be provided to the child for the better health and growth of their body and to enhance their immune system.
  • Caregiving: A child’s health, education, and social interactions all depend on a caregiver’s ability to observe and respond to their physical, psychological, and emotional needs.
  • Security and Safety: Young children are extremely susceptible to a variety of environmental dangers. When a parent doesn’t make them feel safe and secure, their child may become socially awkward and show dread toward both adults and other kids.
  • Opportunities for Early Learning: Education doesn’t stop when a kid goes to daycare, preschool, or school – it starts at home. A child’s motor coordination, social skills, resilience, creativity, and learning capacity will all be enhanced by this early learning. Play, interaction with other kids and adults, and conversation all help to create early learning opportunities.
How can Disney films support a child’s overall development?
