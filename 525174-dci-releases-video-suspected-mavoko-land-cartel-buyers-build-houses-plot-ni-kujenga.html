DCI Releases Video of Suspected Mavoko Land Cartel Asking Buyers to Build Houses: &quot;Plot ni Kujenga&quot;

March 16, 2024
blog
  • The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) is investigating the alleged grabbing of East African Portland Cement Land in Athi River
  • DCI released a video showing how innocent Kenyans were duped into building on the contested land
  • In the viral video, a man believed to be a senior official of a land-selling company told buyers to construct houses as a form of security

Nairobi - The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has released a video of how innocent buyers built houses on the contested East African Portland Cement land in Athi River.

What Mavoko land buyers were told by brokers

According to the DCI, fraudulent land cartels used convincing language to have buyers construct houses on the contested land.

"How developers were duped into building houses on the Portland Cement land by cartels," DCI said on Thursday, October 19.

In the video shared by DCI, a man dressed in a suit is captured threatening land buyers who had not constructed any structure on the piece of land.

The man told the buyers to construct a house or any structure on the land to block those claiming ownership.

He threatened to repossess the land if any buyers failed to construct a structure within a specified period.

"In Kenya, any land without structure is prone to litigations. Even if you have a title deed made of gold or metal, you will keep fighting with people if your land lacks any structure. That is why, for us to win this battle, everyone must construct on his land. People will not make others loss theirs, and that is why we shall repossess those plots that are empty," the man said.

According to the man, believed to be an official of a land-selling company, owning land is about constructing a structure and not merely having a title deed.

"Go to Mombasa; you will realise that empty plots have more than 10 owners. The title deed is just a paper. Land is all about constructing a building," he added.

How Alfred Mutua warned Mavoko land buyers

Meanwhile, former Machakos governor Alfred Mutua in 2015 warned unsuspecting Kenyans against buying the contested land.

Addressing the media on November 13, 2015, Mutua claimed there were plans by senior government officials to grab Portland Cement land.

"I have written to EACC to investigate massive corruption involving land grabbing of 13,000 acres located at Mavoko, Machakos county. We are aware that interested groups, including senior government officials, are involved in the plot to sell the land," Mutua said.

Mutua cautioned the public against buying the land, arguing that land grabbers were conning them.

He advised those who had already purchased the land to vacate and report the matter to the police for investigation.

"I direct that all those occupying the land to vacate; there were never any squatters and the people who are there were recently imported to justice land grabbing. The land is not and has never been for sale; therefore, any person who has purchased the parcel of land should consider themselves to have been conned. I advise them to report to the police," he said.
