Burglars Steal KSh 4 Billion in Cash from Money Holding Facility in One of LA's Largest Money Heist

blog
  • Police are looking for a gang of thieves who carted away KSh 4 billion from a money storage facility in Los Angeles
  • The heist, considered one of the biggest in LA's history, happened over the Easter holidays
  • The theft was discovered when the facility was opened for business, and investigators explained how they breached it

Police have launched a manhunt in Los Angeles, California, after thieves made away with KSh4 billion in cash from a money storage facility.

How did thieves steal KSh 4 billion?

According to the police, the heist, touted as one of the largest in Los Angeles history, occurred on Easter Sunday at a Sylmar, San Fernando Valley facility.

The thieves reportedly entered the storage facility through the roof and breached the vault where the cash was stored.

According to The Los Angeles Times, the burglars only triggered a single alarm, which was not connected to the LAPD, hinting at the possibility of a professional crew.

The facility's owners did not discover the massive theft until they opened the vault Monday for business.

The LAPD and the FBI on Thursday, April 4, 2024, sought tips from the public to unravel the brazen theft.

It comes under two years after thieves stole jewels and gemstones worth over KSh 12 billion from an unattended trailer ferrying the valuables in the San Francisco Bay Area.

The LAPD said the trailer had been travelling overnight from a jewellery show when it stopped at a truck stop around 2am.

The thieves pounced on it and broke the lock after the vehicle’s two armed guards left.
