    What are Saccos?

    Saccos are a type of savings and credit cooperative organization commonly known as a credit union. These organizations are formed by members who pool their financial resources together, allowing them to access credit and other financial services at lower costs than those offered by traditional banks. Saccos are a great way to save money and gain access to credit while also supporting local communities.

    How do Kenyan Saccos work?

    Kenyan Saccos are financial institutions that provide members with access to savings and loan products. They are owned and operated by their members, who are usually people from the same community, and are often organized around a common purpose or interest.

    Members make regular deposits into the Sacco and then can use the pooled funds to take out loans for personal or business needs. The loans are usually provided at low-interest rates, and Sacco also often provides other services such as life insurance and retirement plans.
